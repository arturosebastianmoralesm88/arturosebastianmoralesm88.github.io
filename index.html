About

I am an Agronomist with a Master's degree from Universidad Austral de Chile, currently working as Technical Manager of the Active Germplasm Bank at INIA Carillanca, Temuco. My work spans germplasm conservation, field trials, scientific writing, and computer vision research.

My master's thesis developed an automated pipeline for wheat spike phenotyping using OpenCV and machine learning (SVR, KNN), currently being submitted to The Plant Phenome Journal. I am particularly interested in the intersection of traditional agronomy and modern computational tools.

I work within the INIA-UFRO Phenotyping Group, collaborating on genetic resources evaluation projects for wheat, bromegrass, quinoa, chickpea, and lupine, with a strong focus on efficient use of natural resources.

Research Lines

🌱
Genetic Resources Conservation
Management and characterization of the Active Germplasm Bank at INIA Carillanca. Conservation of wheat, quinoa, bromegrass, chickpea, and lupine accessions with focus on morphological and agronomic descriptors.
👁️
Automated Plant Phenotyping
Development of computer vision pipelines for high-throughput phenotyping of wheat spikes and seeds. Application of machine learning models (SVR, KNN, deep learning) for trait prediction from image data.
💧
Resource Use Efficiency
Evaluation of water and phosphorus use efficiency in wheat and bromegrass genotypes. Identification of cultivars adapted to nutrient-limiting and drought conditions for resilient agriculture in southern Chile.
